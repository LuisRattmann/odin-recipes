Lasagna

Description

LASAGNA DEL NONNO — ITALIAN LASAGNE GRANDPA'S WAY

Ingredients

Ingredients for the dough and topping (serves 4):

  • 500 g lasagne sheets (about 18 sheets)
  • 3 packets of mozzarella
  • 500 g lean, cooked ham
  • 250g grated Parmesan
  • Additionally — if desired: green salad as a side dish

Ingredients for the sugo (sauce):

  • 750 g ground beef
  • 1 large onion
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 1 bunch of basil
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 750 ml tomato passata
  • 1 tbsp broth oil for frying
  • 1/2 tbsp sugar
  • Salt and pepper

Ingredients for the béchamel sauce:

  • 750ml milk (or 300ml milk and 200ml vegetable stock)
  • 45 grams of butter
  • 60 grams of flour
  • lemon juice
  • nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

Prepare the sauce:

  1. Peel and dice 1 large onion
  2. Peel 1 clove of garlic and press through a garlic press
  3. Put some oil in a large saucepan, heat and fry the meat in it (be careful with hot oil)
  4. Add onion pieces and garlic and sauté as well
  5. Add the crushed tomatoes and 3 tablespoons of tomato paste and bring to the boil
  6. Wash a handful of basil leaves, cut into small pieces and add to the sauce
  7. Add 1/2 tablespoon sugar and 1 tablespoon broth
  8. Season with salt and pepper and let simmer for a while

Prepare the béchamel sauce:

  1. Melt butter in a saucepan
  2. stir in the flour in batches (beat vigorously with a whisk!)
  3. then stir in the milk in portions (note: if the sauce is still too thick, feel free to add a little more milk - the bechamel sauce must be nice and creamy at the end)
  4. add a squeeze of lemon juice, season with nutmeg, salt and pepper and mix well again

Prepare more fillings:

  1. Take the mozzarella out of the package and cut into small pieces
  2. Cut 500g cooked ham into pieces

Layer the Lasagna:

  1. Preheat the oven to 180 degrees
  2. Put a little oil on a piece of kitchen paper to rub out a large casserole dish
  3. Pour 2 scoops of the sugo into the casserole dish and spread evenly over the base with a spoon
  4. Place 1 layer of lasagne sheets on top (note: some sheets have to be broken in order to cover the entire sugo with pasta sheets)
  5. Spread another 2 scoops of sugo on the lasagne sheets
  6. Then spread about 1 tablespoon of the bechamel sauce, a few pieces of mozzarella and some ham on the sauce
  7. Always alternately layer sugo, bechamel sauce, mozzarella, ham and 1 layer of lasagne sheets in the casserole dish Tip: only use 1 ladleful of sugo in a smaller casserole dish - the ratio between pasta sheets and sugo must be right, otherwise the lasagne will be soggy
  8. The top layer of the lasagne must contain sugo, béchamel sauce and ham
  9. Finally spread enough mozzarella and 250g grated Parmesan on the lasagne
  10. Cook in the oven for about 30 minutes at 180 degrees (note: when the pasta sheets are soft, the lasagne is ready - it's best to poke it in with a fork and test)

Serving:

  1. Place the casserole dish with the lasagne on the table and spread it out on the plates
  2. If desired, serve with a green salad with olive oil and balsamic vinegar dressing

Buon Appetito! - that's Italian and means "bon appetit"!
